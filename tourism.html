Rhine

The Rhine is not only one of Europe's most beautiful rivers, it's also one of the continent's most important waterways. Flowing some 1,320 kilometers from Switzerland all the way to the Netherlands and the North Sea, this spectacular natural waterway is also one of the most popular river cruising destinations on the planet. One of the most appealing sections of this mighty river flows through Germany's Middle Rhine Valley (Rheintal), an area of outstanding natural beauty. In fact, the stretch of river extending from Mannheim in the south to Koblenz in the north boasts scenery so spectacular that a large section, referred to as the Rhine Gorge (Rheinschlucht), has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Lake Eibsee

Lake Eibsee is an incredibly beautiful lake near Zugspitze in Germany. By all means, it’s one of the most fascinating lakes in the state of Bavaria around 10 km southwest from Garmisch-Partenkirchen and 100 km southwest of Munich. Visiting Eibsee is definitely one of the best day trips from Munich. The most popular activity there is a scenic hike around the lake itself. In this blog, you can read about Lake Eibsee, the hike around the lake, and all the practical information that you need to plan your trip to Eibsee.

Nuremberg

Nuremberg (Nürnberg), Bavaria’s second-largest city and the unofficial capital of Franconia, is an energetic place where the nightlife is intense and the beer is as dark as coffee. As one of Bavaria’s biggest draws it is alive with visitors year-round, but especially during the spectacular Christmas market.

Berlin

Berlin is also an edgy center for avant-garde work in music, the visual and performing arts, where nightclubs like the legendary techno dance hall Berghain are packed with partygoers until dawn. The past is never far from sight and monuments like the Holocaust Memorial and Berlin Wall Memorial dot throughout the city. It is also a city that rebuilt itself with glittering modern architecture like the striking modernist Berliner Philharmonie concert hall with its swooping roof lined in shimmering gold. Shaped by a turbulent past, Berlin never takes itself for granted, and visitors will find a relaxed, fun-loving spirit.
